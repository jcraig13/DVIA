Peering into the illusion of each culture's perception of alcohol. Asking ourselves, "does alcohol have the same effect everywhere?" Does marketing obscure the reality of alcohol?
Using data found, LINK HERE, we will be able to analyze this question by looking at the mental and financial health juxtaposed with each country's alcohol intake.

This data set includes:
  • a country identifier
  • alcohol consumption per adult (15+) in liters recorded and estimated per capita consumption
  • an income per capita in constant 2000 US$, inflation but not the differences in the cost of living between countries has been taken into account
  • employment rate is total employees (15+) percentage of the total population during a given year

Pure Spirit

Smirnoff vodka has dominated the alcohol industry with their advertising. Starting with their "Exclusivity for Everybody Campaign" they sell what is normally for the Czars to the common man. In the mid 90's their slogan is, "The Effect is Shattering". Selling a life of wealth and deviance. Smirnoff has adapted to today's market and capatalizes on the "Smirnoff Ice Challenge" where a victim chugs one of their products. Alcohol marketing captures the insecurities the common person has and sells them the ultimate desires.





It is interesting to see the correlation between alcohol and suicide rate per country. I think this is an interesting way to guage mental health, while including accidental death while intoxicated.
